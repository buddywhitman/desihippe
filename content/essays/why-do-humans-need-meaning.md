A conversation between science, spirituality and civilization. We look at meaning as a public resource - something built in families, libraries, temples, studios, streets and friendships.

### The Search for Purpose

In an age dominated by metrics and efficiency, the human hunger for meaning remains our most stubborn characteristic. We are not merely input-output machines.

* We build sanctuaries of quiet.
* We read old books to find friends who died centuries ago.
* We ask questions that have no financial value.

Meaning is the climate of the human soul. When it degrades, we feel the drought.
