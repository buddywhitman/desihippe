On design, distance and the loneliness of the concrete age. A note from metro platforms, rented rooms, gated colonies and streets that forgot how to host people.

### The Architecture of Isolation

Modern cities are built for transit and transaction, not habitation and hospitality.

- **Commutes as Walls**: The distance we travel to sell our hours isolates us from our neighbors.
- **The Death of Third Places**: Benches require purchases; parks are locked behind security gates; tea stalls are replaced by cafes that charge for presence.
- **Concrete is Silent**: We live closer than ever, yet hear only the hum of air conditioners.

A city must remember the body, or it becomes a beautiful prison.
